# Critique Rubric

Review concepts or implemented UI in this order.

## 1. Task Clarity

- Is it obvious what the user is here to do?
- Does the screen answer the top question fast enough?

## 2. Information Hierarchy

- Do the most important numbers, labels, and statuses dominate the scan path?
- Is secondary information competing with the primary job?

## 3. Action Clarity

- Is the primary action unmistakable?
- Are destructive or commitment actions clearly differentiated?

## 4. State Coverage

- Are loading, empty, pending, error, and success states designed intentionally?
- Does the design acknowledge stale or syncing data where it matters?

## 5. Trust And Policy Visibility

- Are actor, policy, review, risk, freshness, and provenance cues visible before commitment?
- Does the design hide important consequences until too late?

## 6. Density And Responsiveness

- Does the layout stay legible at desktop and mobile breakpoints?
- Do tables, side panels, and control groups collapse cleanly?

## 7. Copy And Terminology

- Is the tone consistent with a trustworthy operational product?
- Are vague labels or soft euphemisms hiding important meaning?

## 8. Consistency And Reuse

- Does the design align with existing Programa Core shell, templates, partials,
  and interaction patterns?
- Is a new pattern justified, or is it novelty without product benefit?

## Output Expectations

- List findings first, ordered by severity.
- Explain the user risk or UX cost, not just the visual preference.
- Propose concrete fixes.
- Hand standards or accessibility compliance checks to `web-design-guidelines` when needed.
