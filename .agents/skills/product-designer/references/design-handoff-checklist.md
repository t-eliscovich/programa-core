# Design Handoff Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing a design brief or redesign brief.

## Problem Framing

- Is the user, task, and success condition explicit?
- Are non-goals named when the request could sprawl?

## Flow And Structure

- Is the entry point into the screen or workflow clear?
- Is the page or step structure explicit?
- Are primary, secondary, and destructive actions named?

## Hierarchy And Content

- Is the information hierarchy concrete enough to implement?
- Are trust signals, policy details, and required review or step-up obligations placed before commitment when relevant?
- Are reuse candidates from local shells or shared primitives called out when helpful?

## States

- Are default, loading, empty, stale, syncing, pending, error, and success states covered where relevant?
- Is the highest-risk failure state handled explicitly?

## Responsive Behavior

- Are desktop, tablet, and mobile expectations clear?
- Are dense tables, multi-panel layouts, and secondary actions adapted for smaller screens?

## Copy And Tone

- Are important labels, terminology, or tone constraints explicit?
- Is any required caution, reassurance, or instructional copy called out?

## Handoff Quality

- Could an implementer build the screen or flow without inventing major UX decisions?
- Are assumptions separated from confirmed decisions?
